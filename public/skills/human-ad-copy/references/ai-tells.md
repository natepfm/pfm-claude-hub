# AI writing tells — full catalog, adapted for ad copy

Source: Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing" (WP:AISIGNS, pulled 2026-07-02), reworked
from its encyclopedia context into direct-response ad copy, plus ad-specific patterns
the Wikipedia page doesn't cover. The scanner script catches sections 1-3 mechanically;
sections 4-6 need human (or Claude) judgment on a reread.

A note on how to read this: one tell in isolation proves nothing (humans use em dashes
too). The catalog matters as a density check. AI copy stacks five of these in a
paragraph. The goal is copy where a suspicious reader finds zero.

## 1. Vocabulary tells

Words that spiked in frequency after 2022 because models overuse them. The overused
word is the tell, not its synonyms, so the fix is never a thesaurus swap. Rewrite the
sentence around a concrete detail instead.

| AI word/phrase | What to do instead |
|---|---|
| delve, dive deep, deep dive | Just say what it is. ("dive in" alone is Caleb diction, allowed) |
| crucial, pivotal, vital, key (adj.) | Say why it matters with a number |
| seamless(ly), effortless(ly), frictionless | Name what friction was removed ("no CSV exports") |
| unlock, unleash, elevate, empower, supercharge, harness | Use the actual verb: build, scrape, launch, save |
| game-changing, game-changer, revolutionary, groundbreaking, transformative, cutting-edge, next-level | Banned (also on Caleb's guru list). Give the receipt |
| leverage, utilize | use |
| landscape, realm, space, ecosystem (abstract) | Name the actual thing ("Meta ads", "your account") |
| tapestry, testament, underscore(s), showcase, boasts | Delete or state plainly |
| journey | Skip it |
| robust, comprehensive, holistic | The number or the list of what's included |
| meticulous(ly), intricate, intricacies | Say what the care actually produced |
| foster(ing), garner, bolster | Plain verb |
| actionable insights, valuable insights | The actual takeaway, stated |
| streamline, optimize your workflow | What specifically gets faster, and by how much ("optimize" alone is legit Meta vocabulary when literal: "optimize for purchases") |
| say goodbye to X, say hello to Y | State the change once, concretely |
| look no further, we've got you covered, the possibilities are endless | Delete |
| in today's..., fast-paced, ever-evolving, digital age | Delete the whole opener |

Era note: the specific overused words drift as models update (delve peaked in 2023-24,
"showcasing/highlighting" dominate newer models). The stable pattern is inflated
abstract vocabulary in place of specifics; new buzzwords that feel like these belong
on this list even before anyone documents them.

## 2. Sentence-pattern tells

**Negative parallelism.** The loudest tell in current ad copy. All variants:
- "It's not just X, it's Y" / "isn't just another X"
- "Not only X, but also Y"
- "It's not about X. It's about Y."
- "No X. No Y. Just Z."
- "X rather than Y" (stacked repeatedly)

Humans occasionally write one of these. AI ad copy is built out of them. Rewrite as
the positive claim, standing alone: "This isn't another course, it's a community of
practitioners" becomes "529 media buyers running real budgets, on calls together
twice a month."

**Rule of three.** Exactly three adjectives, three parallel phrases, three benefit
bullets, over and over. Models use triads to make thin analysis look complete. Cut to
two or extend to four, or make the items structurally uneven.

**Copula avoidance.** "Serves as", "stands as", "acts as", "marks", "represents",
"offers a", "features a" where a person would write "is" or "has". Measured effect:
AI text uses is/are over 10% less than human text. Write "is" and "has" freely.

**Participle tails.** A benefit clause glued to the sentence end with a comma:
", ensuring consistent results", ", helping you scale faster", ", allowing you to
focus on what matters". This is superficial analysis in miniature. If the benefit is
real, it earns its own sentence and its own receipt.

**Rhetorical-fragment pivots.** "The result? 3x ROAS." "The best part? It's free."
Punchy-sounding, and a strong tell because models learned it from a decade of
LinkedIn posts. Just make the statement.

**Vague attribution.** "Top media buyers agree", "experts say", "industry reports
show", "trusted by professionals everywhere". Weasel wording. Either name the person
and quote (with permission) or cut.

**Hedged grandeur.** "One of the most powerful approaches available today." AI avoids
checkable commitments while inflating tone. Humans commit to verifiable specifics:
"first", "only", "529 members", "$97/month". If it's true and checkable, say it flat;
if you can't check it, don't claim it.

**Elegant variation.** Calling the same thing by a new name every mention (the
community, this ecosystem, your new home base, the collective) because models penalize
repetition. Humans repeat the natural name. Call it "the community" every time.

## 3. Formatting tells

- **Em and en dashes.** Banned outright in this hub (house rule), and AI's
  space-padded " — " usage is a documented tell. Commas, periods, parentheses, colons.
- **Curly quotes and apostrophes** (“ ” ‘ ’) instead of straight ones: paste artifact
  from a chatbot. Use straight quotes.
- **Bold inline-header bullets.** `- **Speed:** launch ads in minutes` repeated down a
  list. The signature AI list format. Use Caleb's plain dashes or "1.) 2.) 3.)" style
  with normal sentences.
- **Emoji as list bullets** or one emoji per line, mechanically. Caleb sprinkles one
  or two per post at a beat or CTA, never as scaffolding.
- **Title Case On Every Heading.** Caleb writes sentence case.
- **Boldface sprayed on key phrases** throughout a paragraph, takeaway-style.
- **Perfectly parallel list items**, every bullet the same grammatical shape and
  length. Human lists wobble.

## 4. Tone tells

- **Puffery / significance inflation.** "A vibrant community with a rich heritage of
  excellence." Generic positive descriptions that could apply to anything are the
  regression-to-the-mean signature. Every sentence should contain something that only
  applies to this specific offer.
- **Press-release voice.** "Kruse Media Solutions is committed to empowering media
  buyers through innovative AI solutions." Nobody talks like this, least of all Caleb.
- **Frictionless enthusiasm.** Everything is amazing, nothing has a rough edge. Real
  practitioners admit mess: "still working through some bugs", "took me three tries",
  "my one big fear is". At least one line of honest friction makes copy read human
  and is on-voice anyway.
- **Grand-challenge framing.** "In an era where advertisers face mounting
  challenges..." The Wikipedia "Despite challenges... future prospects" formula in ad
  clothing. Open with the specific pain instead: "Your CPA doubled in March and
  nobody can tell you why."

## 5. What human copy does (write toward these)

From the "Signs of human writing" research plus Caleb's real corpus:

- Simple is/has constructions, used without embarrassment.
- Plain verbs: use, built, tried, wrote, moved, spent.
- Definitive, checkable claims and superlatives when true ("the first", "the only
  one I've found that").
- Natural hedges and intensifiers: "pretty much", "honestly", "very", "tends to".
- Occasional wordiness: "the fact that", "in order to". Humans aren't maximally
  compressed. Don't inject errors on purpose; just don't sand every edge.
- Specifics that could only come from doing the thing: prices, error messages, tool
  names with versions, what broke, how long it took.
- Varied rhythm: fragments, a long rambling sentence, a two-word one.
- Personality artifacts where the voice doc allows: ":)", "haha", "for ya",
  parenthetical asides.

## 6. Before / after examples

**Example 1 — community ad primary text**

AI slop:
> Tired of juggling countless tools? Meet The Ai Ads Alchemists — a vibrant community
> of elite media buyers leveraging cutting-edge AI to transform their workflows. It's
> not just another course. It's a complete ecosystem designed to elevate your media
> buying, unlock explosive growth, and future-proof your career. Say goodbye to
> creative bottlenecks. The best part? It's only $97/month. 🚀

Human (Caleb voice):
> Last month I built a Claude Code agent that scrapes every ad my competitors are
> running into an Airtable base. Took about 2 hours. I hand tools like that to the
> 529 media buyers in my community every week, free, with the build recorded so you
> can copy it. $97/month. If you're running real budgets and your creative testing
> is stuck, come hang out.

Why it works: one verifiable build story, real numbers (2 hours, 529, $97), plain
verbs, no parallelism, admits nothing fancy, ends with a low-friction ask.

**Example 2 — headline (~40 chars)**

AI slop: `Unlock Your Creative Edge With AI`
Human: `Scrape any competitor's ads in 2 hrs`

**Example 3 — hook line**

AI slop:
> In today's ever-evolving advertising landscape, standing out isn't just
> important — it's essential.

Human:
> Your CPA doubled in March and Meta support sent you an article about pixel setup.

**Example 4 — the thesaurus trap (what NOT to do when fixing)**

Flagged: "Seamlessly integrates with your existing workflow."
Bad fix (synonym swap, still AI-shaped): "Smoothly connects with your current setup."
Real fix (receipt): "Syncs your Airtable base to Meta in one click. No CSV exports,
no copy-pasting IDs."
